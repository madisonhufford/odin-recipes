Restraunt Style Spanish Rice

Description

Make authentic Mexican rice just like you get in a Mexican restaurant. Light and fluffy and slightly smoky, this is great as a side to tacos or enchiladas or use as an ingredient in homemade burritos. You'll never buy Spanish rice in a pouch or box again!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long grain white rice
  • 1/8 small onion
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 3/4 cups water, divided
  • 1 tsp Knorr caldo de pollo
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3 Tbsp corn oil

Steps

  1. In a blender or food processor, puree tomato paste, onion and garlic with 1/4 cup water.
  2. In a medium heavy bottomed saucepan, heat corn oil over medium-high heat. When it's hot, add rice.
  3. Cook, stirring constantly, until golden brown and smells toasty.
  4. Add tomato paste puree to rice and cook, s tirring constantly 1 minute.
  5. Add in remaining 1 1/2 cups water, caldo de pollo and salt. Bring to a boil.
  6. Once boiling, cover, turn heat down to lowest possible setting and simmer 20 minutes. Do not lift lid!
  7. When 20 minutes is up, turn heat off, lift lid to release excess steam and heat and recover. Let rest 10 more minutes.
  8. When 10 minutes is up, fluff rice with a fork and serve.
